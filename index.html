About This Guide

Hi, I'm Abby deVarennes — a wine enthusiast and fellow TACC traveler, not a sommelier or wine expert. This guide is a collection of information I've compiled, tasted, and learned along the way while living or traveling across wine-rich regions. It's meant to be helpful, approachable, and practical for bringing wine home to enjoy — not intimidating.

Each section is organized into what you can find on base (typically limited selections from Class Six, shoppettes, or BX/PX) and off-base options — nearby wine shops, grocery stores, and boutique spots worth checking out. My goal is to help others buy great wine without overspending, whether you're in California, Spain, Germany, Crete, or wherever the grapes grow.

Cheers to finding something good in every glass.

How Will I Know What I Like?

Wine can feel overwhelming, but it doesn't have to be. Start with what sounds good to you — bold and rich, or light and crisp? This chart shows red wines from lightest to boldest to help you explore.

Red Wine Boldness

← LighterBolder →
Light-Bodied: Pinot Noir, Gamay, Schiava
Medium-Bodied: Grenache, Merlot, Sangiovese, Tempranillo, Zinfandel
Full-Bodied: Cabernet Sauvignon, Syrah, Malbec, Mourvèdre
Boldest: Petite Sirah, Sagrantino, Tannat, Touriga Nacional

Cool climate wines tend lighter; warm climate wines tend bolder.

The best way to find what you like? Try a few bottles from different regions and grapes. Start with the "Class 6 BX" picks in each region — they're approachable, affordable, and widely available on base.
